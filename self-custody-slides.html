"Not your keys,
not your coins."

When your bitcoin sits on an exchange, you hold an IOU. The exchange holds the keys. If they go down, your bitcoin goes with them.

Mt. Gox. 850,000 BTC lost. FTX. Billions gone. Celsius. Frozen withdrawals. The pattern is clear.

What are you actually protecting?

  • A private key ‚Äî a 256-bit number that proves ownership of your bitcoin
  • Whoever has the key can spend the coins. No customer support. No reversals.
  • Self-custody means you ‚Äî and only you ‚Äî control that key

Think of it like a combination to a vault. Anyone with the combination can open it. There's no locksmith to call.

Protecting your seed phrase

Do

  • Write it on paper or stamp into metal
  • Store in a fireproof, waterproof location
  • Consider splitting across locations
  • Test your backup by recovering on a spare device

Don't

  • Take a photo or screenshot
  • Store in a notes app or cloud drive
  • Email or text it to yourself
  • Leave it in plain sight at home

Best practices checklist

  • Buy hardware wallets directly from the manufacturer ‚Äî never secondhand or from marketplace sellers
  • Verify firmware and software ‚Äî always update from official sources
  • Use a dedicated device ‚Äî don't install wallet software on a shared or compromised computer
  • Practice with small amounts first ‚Äî send a small test transaction before loading real value
  • Plan for inheritance ‚Äî trusted family members need instructions (not just the seed)

The "25th word":
BIP-39 passphrases

  • An optional passphrase added on top of your 12/24-word seed
  • Creates an entirely different wallet ‚Äî different keys, different addresses, different funds
  • Same seed + different passphrase = completely separate wallet with no visible link

Plausible deniability: You can keep a decoy balance on the passphrase-free wallet and your real stack behind the passphrase. Under duress, reveal only the base seed.

The risk: If you forget or lose the passphrase, those funds are gone. There is no recovery. The seed alone won't find them.

Remember these

  • Self-custody eliminates counterparty risk ‚Äî your keys, your rules
  • Hardware wallets keep keys offline ‚Äî away from malware and hackers
  • Your seed phrase is everything ‚Äî protect it physically, test your backup
  • Passphrases are powerful but dangerous ‚Äî forget it and funds are lost
  • Start small, build confidence ‚Äî then scale up
